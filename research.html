Research themes

Single photon sources

Single photons act as essential information carriers in quantum networks, traveling through fiber links. Simultaneously, entangled photon pairs deliver the vital quantum correlations required for secure key distribution and teleportation between distant nodes.

Quantum memory based on rare earth ions

Rare-earth ions act as highly efficient quantum memories due to their exceptionally long coherence times. They enable the critical storage and synchronized retrieval of photons necessary to bridge distant nodes in scalable quantum networks.

Quantum imaging and metrology

Quantum imaging and metrology harness the strict spatial and spectral correlations of entangled photon pairs. By utilizing coincidence detection, these techniques dramatically suppress background noise and achieve phase sensitivity far surpassing classical limits.

Hybrid quantum network

Hybrid quantum networks seamlessly integrate distinct physical platforms, like telecom photons for transmission and rare-earth ions for storage. This synergy leverages their unique strengths to build scalable, robust architectures for global quantum communication.

Atomic defect centers in diamond

Atomic defect centers in diamond offer robust stationary qubits for advanced quantum processing. Applying precise optical and microwave pulses enables the high-fidelity initialization, coherent manipulation, and accurate readout of these highly stable spin states.
